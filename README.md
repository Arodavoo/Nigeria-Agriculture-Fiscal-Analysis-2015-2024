# Nigeria-Agriculture-Fiscal-Analysis-2015-2024
Evaluating whether increased public spending in Nigeria’s agriculture sector translated into growth and price stability (2015–2024).

## Overview

> **Core question:** *Do higher public allocations to agriculture translate into stronger sector growth and better food price stability?*

This project is a **10-year data review** of Nigeria’s federal agriculture allocation and sector performance between **2015 and 2024**.

Instead of building a dashboard, the focus is on **structured data cleaning, inflation adjustment, per capita normalization, and trend analysis**.

**Goal:** Examine whether increases in public spending on agriculture were associated with improvements in agricultural growth and food price stability.

---

## 🔎 Key Questions Explored

- How much has the agriculture budget increased over the last decade?
- Has agriculture’s share of total federal spending changed?
- What happens when we adjust allocations for inflation?
- How much allocation does this represent per Nigerian?
- Did higher spending correspond with higher agricultural GDP growth?
- How has food inflation behaved over the same period?

---

## 📚 Data Sources

All data used in this analysis were publicly available:

- **Federal Budget Office of Nigeria** — Approved Appropriation Acts  
- **National Bureau of Statistics (NBS)** — GDP Reports & CPI Reports  
- **World Bank** — Population Data  

---

## 🛠 Methodology

1. Extracted annual agriculture budget (**Recurrent + Capital**)
2. Converted nominal allocations to **real values** using CPI
3. Calculated:
   - Capital allocation ratio
   - Agriculture share of total federal budget
   - Real per capita agriculture allocation
   - Budget growth rates
4. Compared fiscal trends with:
   - Agricultural GDP growth
   - Food inflation trends

✅ All cleaning and calculations were done in **Excel**.  


---

## 📈 Selected Findings (2015–2024)

- Nominal agriculture allocation increased from **₦40.7bn** to **₦996.9bn**
- Agriculture’s share of total federal spending rose from **0.9%** to **3.5%**
- Real per capita allocation increased significantly over the decade
- Agricultural GDP growth averaged **~2.5%**
- Food inflation rose from **9.9%** to **39.1%**

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> The data suggests that higher allocations coincided with **modest output growth** and **rising food inflation** over the period.
